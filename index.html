Before you film anything

  1. You don't need to post every day. Three a week is plenty. Pick a pace you can hold for months.
  2. Batch it. Film 3 or 4 videos in one relaxed hour, once a week. Same spot, same hoodie, phone propped up. One sitting covers the week.
  3. Film once, post three times. The same clip usually works on TikTok, Reels and Facebook. The labels on each card tell you where it fits.
  4. Bullet points, not scripts. Know your hook and two or three things to say, then hit record. First take is usually the keeper.
  5. Start with the green “Quick win” cards. Screen records, voiceovers, no-talking stuff. Some don't even need your face.
  6. Done beats perfect. Phone camera, daylight, saying something honest. The scruffy ones are the ones that feel real.

TikTok
  • Raw beats polished. Film on your phone, use TikTok's own text and captions.
  • The hook lands in the first second. Keep most videos 15 to 45s.
  • 1 to 3 hashtags is plenty. Turn good comments into reply videos.
  • Old videos can blow up weeks later. Don't delete the "flops".
Instagram
  • Reels take the same videos. A slightly tidier edit and a decent cover frame help the grid.
  • Carousels for screenshots and monthly recaps. Best save rate of anything.
  • 3 to 5 hashtags. The caption can carry a bit more story here.
  • Saves and shares beat likes. End with something worth keeping.
Facebook
  • Longer captions work. Tell the story in text above the video.
  • Subtitles always on. Most Facebook video plays silent.
  • Shares are the win. Myth-busting and "tag a mate" stuff travels furthest.
  • Repost your Reels here as they are. Local groups are an underrated boost.
Batch filming day, the 20-minute checklist
  1. Pick 3 or 4 cards before you touch the camera. Mix at least one Quick win in.
  2. Copy the hooks into your notes app. That's your teleprompter.
  3. Set up once: phone propped at chest height, facing a window, lens wiped.
  4. Film everything back to back. One take each unless it genuinely fell apart.
  5. Name the clips after the card titles so future you isn't confused.
  6. Post roughly one a day. Don't dump them all at once. Tick Filmed here as you go.
The 5-minute Friday review, how to know what's working
  1. Find your best video of the week on each app. Views are fine as a first pass.
  2. Ask why it won. Was the hook a question, a number, or an honest admission? Film two more in that style next week.
  3. Rate saves and shares above likes. A video with 40 saves beat one with 400 passive likes.
  4. The winner rule: if something pops off, post a part 2 within a few days while people are still looking.
  5. Don't judge anything in under a week. TikTok especially revives videos late.
  6. Keep score in the group chat. One line each Friday: best video, plus your guess at why. After a month the pattern writes next month's plan for you.
