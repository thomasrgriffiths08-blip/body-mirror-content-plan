New to posting? Read this first — it's the whole game

  1. You don't need to post every day. Three videos a week is plenty. Pick the pace you can keep up for months — consistency beats volume every time.
  2. Batch it. Film 3–4 videos in one relaxed hour, once a week. Same spot, same hoodie, phone propped up. One session = a whole week of posts, zero daily pressure.
  3. Film once, post three times. The same video usually works on TikTok, Reels and Facebook. The labels on each card show where it fits — one film day feeds all three.
  4. Bullet points, not scripts. Know your hook and 2–3 things to say, then press record. First take is usually the best take.
  5. Start with the green “Quick win” cards. Filter Effort → Quick win. Screen records, voiceovers and no-talking formats — some don't even need your face.
  6. Done beats perfect. Phone camera + daylight + saying something honest outperforms a polished ad. The scruffy videos are the ones that feel real.

TikTok
  • Raw beats polished — film on your phone and use TikTok's own text & captions.
  • The hook lands in the first second. Keep most videos 15–45s.
  • 1–3 hashtags is plenty. Turn good comments into reply videos.
  • It's a discovery machine — old videos can pop off weeks later. Don't delete "flops".
Instagram
  • Reels = the same videos; a slightly tidier edit and a decent cover frame help the grid.
  • Use carousels for screenshots and monthly recaps — the highest save-rate format.
  • 3–5 hashtags; the caption can carry a bit more story here.
  • Saves and shares beat likes — end videos with something worth keeping.
Facebook
  • Longer captions work — tell the story in text above the video.
  • Subtitles always on: most Facebook video plays silently.
  • Shares are the win — myth-busting and "tag a mate" content travels furthest.
  • Repost your Reels here as-is; local groups are an underrated boost.
Batch filming day — the 20-minute checklist
  1. Pick 3–4 cards before you touch the camera (mix at least one Quick win in).
  2. Copy the hooks into your notes app — that's your teleprompter.
  3. Set up once: phone propped at chest height, facing a window, lens wiped.
  4. Film everything back to back. One take each unless it genuinely fell apart.
  5. Name the clips after the card titles so future-you isn't confused.
  6. Post roughly one per day — don't dump them all at once. Tick Filmed here as you go.
The 5-minute Friday review — how to know what's working
  1. Find your best video of the week on each app (views are fine as a first pass).
  2. Ask why it won: was the hook a question, a number, or an honest admission? Film two more in that style next week.
  3. Rate saves & shares above likes. A video with 40 saves beat one with 400 passive likes — the algorithms agree.
  4. The winner rule: if something pops off, post a Part 2 within a few days while people are still looking.
  5. Don't judge anything in under a week. TikTok especially revives videos weeks later.
  6. Keep score in the group chat: one line each Friday — best video + your guess at why. After a month, the pattern writes next month's plan for you.
